Optimizing Polynomial Models of Algebraic Curves

In cryptographic applications, we are interested in elliptic curves that have a specified number of points. One way to do this involves an algebraic curve, the modular curve X1(N) [1]. However, the polynomial defining X1(N) may be extremely large, with tens of thousands of terms, say. Fortunately, to facilitate computations, it may be possible to find a change of coordinates that simplifies the polynomial considerably.

In this project, my mentor Andrew V. Sutherland and I developed a non-deterministic algorithm, using simulated annealing, which searches for simpler polynomials by repeatedly applying a small set of atomic operations. Our algorithm matched and in many cases improved on previously known results, with very reasonable runtime: for instance, simplifying a polynomial with total degree 352 and 33,618 terms (representing X1(101)) required less than 3 minutes on a standard desktop.

You can find the polynomial optimization code (in C) here and a poster describing the project here.

[1] A. V. Sutherland, "Computing Hilbert class polynomials with the Chinese Remainder Theorem." Mathematics of Computation 80 (2011).
